Komm, Süsser Tod

Komm, Süsser Tod, destruction in music

Music in cinema has the power to transcend words and images, provoke deep emotions and reinforce the impact of a scene. "Komm, Süsser Tod," the iconic song from the soundtrack of the Japanese animated film "The End of Evangelion," is a striking example of this ability. Composed by Shiro Sagisu and performed by Arianne, this piece of music plays a crucial role in the work, offering a complex and unsettling emotional experience for the viewer. In this article, we delve into the world of "Komm, Süsser Tod," exploring its music, lyrics and use in the film.

"Komm, Süsser Tod"

"Komm, Süsser Tod" (German for "Come, sweet death") is a fascinating, melancholy song distinguished by its blend of musical styles. It skilfully blends elements of rock, classical and electronic music to create a unique atmosphere. The lyrics, sung in English, are poignant and enigmatic, evoking themes of the end of the world, acceptance and redemption.

The music begins slowly with sublime strings and choirs, creating a mystical, almost religious atmosphere. It then evolves into a more rhythmic, rocking section, with skilful use of electric guitar and drums. This fusion of styles creates a musical tension that reflects the complex emotions of the characters and the story.

How it's used

"Komm, Süsser Tod" is used in a crucial scene in "The End of Evangelion" that serves as the climax of the plot. Without going into detail to avoid spoilers, the song accompanies a visually and emotionally stunning sequence. It amplifies the intensity of the action and the emotions of the characters. The juxtaposition of music and images creates an unforgettable dramatic tension that leaves a lasting impression on the viewer.

The choice of this song for this sequence is particularly apt, as the lyrics of "Komm, Süsser Tod" reflect the film's profound themes. They evoke resignation and loss, but also a glimmer of hope. The music accompanies the characters on an existential and metaphysical journey, creating a striking emotional contrast.

À retenir

"Komm, Süsser Tod" is much more than just a movie song. It is an essential part of the experience of "The End of Evangelion." The music, with its bold fusion of styles and evocative lyrics, creates a unique atmosphere that intensifies the narrative and the characters' emotions.

Its use within the film is a perfect demonstration of how music can become a character in its own right, profoundly influencing how the audience perceives and understands the story. In the end, "Komm, Süsser Tod" remains etched in the memory of moviegoers as an unforgettable melody of apocalypse and redemption.
