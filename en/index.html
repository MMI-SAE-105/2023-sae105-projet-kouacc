The fate of destruction is also the joy of rebirth.

-Keel Lorentz, The End of Evangelion

The End of Evangelion succeeded not only in revolutionizing a genre in decline, but also in revolutionizing Japanese animation. Through psychological and social themes, with the end of the world as its only fatality, this film touches sensitive points about human nature, while giving a true ending to a mythical series.

"The fate of destruction is also the joy of rebirth" resonates throughout the film. Find out how this simple line became much more than just the denouement of the story.
